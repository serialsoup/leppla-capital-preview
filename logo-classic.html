Classic pyramid direction

This follows the reference more directly: a bold pyramid above a serious serif wordmark. The newer directions add subtle layers and a restrained shooting-star accent so the mark feels more ownable without becoming busy.

Keep One strong shape

The pyramid should be blunt, simple, and confident. No internal tiers or diagram details.

Fix Typography does the work

The wordmark needs to carry the firm. The symbol should sit above it quietly.

Watch Pyramid-scheme risk

The safer versions use a single solid silhouette or a flattened cap, not stacked levels.
