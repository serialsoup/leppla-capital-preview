Retirement Planning

How much should you really be saving for retirement?

A practical look at savings rates, timeline, flexibility, and the planning questions that matter more than a single rule of thumb.

Five-minute readRetirement PlanningResources

The best savings target depends on time horizon, spending needs, taxes, and the kind of retirement you want. A useful plan starts by translating future income needs into decisions you can make today.

Start with the life you want, then build the savings rhythm around it.

For many families, the number matters less than the system: save consistently, review annually, keep risk aligned with the time horizon, and avoid making long-term decisions from short-term market stress.
